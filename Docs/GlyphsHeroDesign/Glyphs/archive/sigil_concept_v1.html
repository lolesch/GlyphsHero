Item Chain System

A visual sigil language — each component type carries a distinct shape grammar

Component Shape Language
Reactor Listens for external event

Weapon Chain root — defines the attack

Converter Changes signal downstream
Payload Extra effect fires alongside
Condition Gates everything downstream
Sigil Stacking — Chain Reads as a Single Rune

When items connect in the inventory, their sigils layer on top of each other to form a composite rune on the unit card. Someone unfamiliar reads a unique shape. Someone familiar reads each layer independently.

The outer frame encodes component category. The inner symbol encodes its specific function. The color survives layering as a tint. A glowing center marks the chain root. Read the layers separately to understand it, or read the shape as a whole to recognize it at a glance.

Example Chain — Reading the Sentence
When ally attacks
Strike (10 dmg)
+50% damage
→ Fire damage
Apply Burn

"When an ally attacks → strike for 15 fire damage → and apply Burn"
The chain reads as a sentence. The composite rune on the unit card is the compressed summary of that sentence.
